Societal inequality in 2018

After years of progress, an increasingly negative trend.

Inequality as a systemic problem

According to the Economic Policy Institute CEOs made roughly 24 times the average production workers salary in 1965. In 2009 CEOs salary was more than 185 times that of the average hourly employee. This seems to effect certain populations more than others, as according to the Department of Housing and Urban Development homeless disproportionately effects males, African Americans, veterans and disabled peoples.

Inequality in education and opportunity

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics only those with a four year college degree have experienced a net wage increase since 1979 when adjusted for inflation. While high school drop out rates are similar among races and genders, African Americans are far less likely to enroll in a four year college. (Digest of Education Statistics)

Inequality: An ever increasing problem

All of this is only worsened by the fact that since 1980 the percentage of young black men without a high school diploma that were incarcerated tripled to 37 percent.
